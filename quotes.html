A programming environment is a user interface for understanding a program. Especially in an environment for learning, the environment must be designed to explain. -- Bret Victor, Learnable Programming

Many tools -- the REPL, debuggers and profilers -- arise naturally as responses to questions of the form "How can my programming environment explain X?". For a debugger, X = "what happened during the crash". For a profiler, X = "what's taking time during my program". For the REPL, X is more general and less specific, since a REPL can be used as a general purpose way of interrogating a program for explanations.

What other tools can be inspired by demanding new types of explanations?

Q: How have your clerkships with Posner and Scalia, respectively, shaped your worldview? Posner was the most important person (after my dad) in my life. He showed me the life of someone not afraid to be hated. -- Lawrence Lessig, responding to a question on Reddit

When people respond negatively to us, we usually worry that we're doing something wrong. But maybe we should not only not fear such negative responses. Maybe sometimes we should celebrate them as a sign that we're doing something right.
